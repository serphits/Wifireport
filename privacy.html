Privacy Policy

Last updated: December 22, 2025

TL;DR: WiFi.Report is designed with privacy as a core principle. All network analysis is performed locally in your browser. We don't collect, store, or transmit your personal data or network information to our servers.

1. Introduction

Welcome to WiFi.Report ("we," "our," or "us"). We are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring that your personal information is handled in a safe and responsible manner. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your information when you use our website and services.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide

WiFi.Report is designed to operate entirely in your browser. We do not require you to create an account or provide any personal information to use our services.

2.2 Automatically Collected Information

When you use WiFi.Report, our website may automatically collect certain technical information, including:

  • Browser Information: Browser type, version, and settings
  • Device Information: Device type, operating system, and screen resolution
  • Usage Data: Pages visited, time spent on pages, and navigation patterns
  • Network Analysis Data: Connection speed, latency, and other performance metrics (processed locally only)

2.3 Network Analysis Data

Important: All network analysis performed by WiFi.Report happens locally in your browser using standard web APIs. Your network data, including:

  • IP address information
  • Connection speed and latency measurements
  • Network security assessments
  • Privacy evaluations

...is processed entirely on your device and is never transmitted to our servers.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the limited information we collect for the following purposes:

  • Service Delivery: To provide and maintain our WiFi analysis service
  • Service Improvement: To understand how users interact with our website and improve functionality
  • Analytics: To analyze usage patterns and optimize performance (using aggregated, anonymized data)
  • Security: To protect against fraud, abuse, and security threats

4. Data Storage and Security

4.1 Local Storage

WiFi.Report may use your browser's local storage to:

  • Remember your cookie consent preferences
  • Store temporary analysis results for your convenience
  • Cache resources for better performance

This data is stored locally on your device and can be cleared at any time through your browser settings.

4.2 Security Measures

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your information, including:

  • HTTPS encryption for all communications
  • Regular security assessments
  • Secure coding practices
  • Limited data collection

5. Third-Party Services

WiFi.Report may use limited third-party services for:

  • IP Geolocation: We may use third-party APIs (such as ipapi.co) to provide approximate location information based on your IP address. This is done only when you initiate a scan and is used to display your general location.
  • Analytics: We may use privacy-focused analytics services to understand website usage patterns without tracking individual users.

These services have their own privacy policies, and we encourage you to review them.

6. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

WiFi.Report uses minimal cookies and similar tracking technologies. For detailed information about our cookie usage, please see our Cookie Policy.

7. Your Rights and Choices

You have the following rights regarding your information:

  • Access: Since we don't store your personal data, there is no stored data to access
  • Deletion: You can clear all local storage through your browser settings at any time
  • Opt-Out: You can decline cookies and disable certain features through your browser settings
  • Do Not Track: We respect browser "Do Not Track" signals

8. Children's Privacy

WiFi.Report is not directed to children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe we have collected information from a child, please contact us immediately.

9. International Users

WiFi.Report is accessible globally. By using our service, you acknowledge that your information may be processed in countries with different data protection laws than your own. We are committed to protecting your privacy regardless of your location.

10. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or applicable laws. We will notify users of significant changes by:

  • Posting a notice on our website
  • Updating the "Last Updated" date at the top of this policy

We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically.

11. Data Retention

Since WiFi.Report does not collect or store your personal data or network analysis results on our servers, there is no data retention period. Any data stored in your browser's local storage persists until you clear it.

12. Contact Us

If you have any questions, concerns, or requests regarding this Privacy Policy or our privacy practices, please contact us at:

  • Email: privacy@wifi.report
  • Website: https://wifi.report

13. Legal Basis for Processing (GDPR)

For users in the European Economic Area (EEA), our legal bases for processing your information include:

  • Consent: When you give us permission to use cookies or other technologies
  • Legitimate Interests: To provide, improve, and secure our services
  • Legal Obligation: To comply with applicable laws and regulations

14. California Privacy Rights (CCPA)

California residents have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), including:

  • The right to know what personal information is collected
  • The right to know whether personal information is sold or disclosed
  • The right to opt-out of the sale of personal information
  • The right to request deletion of personal information
  • The right to non-discrimination for exercising CCPA rights

Note: WiFi.Report does not sell personal information and collects minimal data.

Privacy by Design: WiFi.Report was built from the ground up with privacy as a fundamental principle. We believe in transparency and giving users full control over their data. That's why everything happens in your browser, and nothing leaves your device without your explicit knowledge.
