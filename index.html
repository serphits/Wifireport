üìñ Understanding Your Metrics

Download Speed: How fast data comes to your device. Higher is better for streaming, downloading files, and browsing.
Upload Speed: How fast data leaves your device. Important for video calls, uploading files, and live streaming.
Latency (Ping): The time it takes for data to travel to a server and back. Lower is better, especially for gaming and video calls. Good: <50ms, Fair: 50-100ms, Poor: >100ms.
Jitter: Variation in latency over time. Lower is better for stable connections. Good: <10ms, Fair: 10-30ms, Poor: >30ms. High jitter causes stuttering in calls and games.
RTT (Round Trip Time): Similar to latency, measured by your browser. Shows the time for a complete network round trip.
Effective Type: Your estimated connection speed class (2G, 3G, 4G, 5G) based on actual performance, not just what your carrier claims.
Test Method: Optimized multi-flow testing within ~30 seconds for accurate results across devices (similar to fast.com behavior).
