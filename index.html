Understanding Your Metrics & Common Issues

Download Speed: How fast data comes to your device. Higher is better for streaming, downloading files, and browsing.
Common Causes of Slow Download:
  • Distance from router or weak signal strength
  • Too many devices connected to the network
  • ISP throttling or plan limitations
  • Old router hardware or outdated firmware
  • Interference from other WiFi networks or devices
Upload Speed: How fast data leaves your device. Important for video calls, uploading files, and live streaming.
Common Causes of Slow Upload:
  • Asymmetric internet plans (typically lower upload speeds)
  • Network congestion during peak hours
  • Background uploads or cloud sync services
  • Router quality of service (QoS) settings
Response Time (Latency): The time it takes for data to travel to a server and back. Lower is better, especially for gaming and video calls. Good: <50ms, Fair: 50–100ms, Poor: >100ms.
Common Causes of Slow Response Time:
  • Using WiFi instead of wired connection
  • Geographic distance from the server
  • Network congestion or heavy traffic
  • Poor quality router or modem
  • ISP routing issues or network problems
Signal Variation (Jitter): How much your connection speed varies over time. Lower is better for stable connections. Good: <10ms, Fair: 10–30ms, Poor: >30ms.
Common Causes of Connection Variation:
  • WiFi signal fluctuations or interference
  • Network congestion from multiple devices
  • Inconsistent ISP routing or buffering
  • Old or failing network hardware
  • Distance from access point or obstacles
Security Score: Measures your connection's encryption and security protocols.
Common Causes of Low Security:
  • Using HTTP instead of HTTPS for the current site
  • Outdated browser or security settings
  • Public WiFi without VPN protection
  • Weak or no encryption on router (WEP/open network)
  • Missing security headers or policies
Privacy Score: Indicates whether your real IP address is exposed or protected.
Common Causes of Privacy Issues:
  • Not using a VPN service
  • WebRTC leaks revealing your real IP
  • DNS requests not encrypted or routed through ISP
  • Browser fingerprinting vulnerabilities
  • Trackers and third-party cookies enabled
