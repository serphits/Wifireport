Cookie Policy

Last updated: December 29, 2025

Quick Summary: WiFi.Report uses cookies for essential functionality, Google Analytics (to understand site usage), and Google AdSense (for advertising). We don't track individual users personally, don't sell your data, and all network analysis happens locally in your browser.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, smartphone, or tablet) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners.

Cookies can be "persistent" (remaining on your device for a set period) or "session" (deleted when you close your browser).

2. How We Use Cookies

WiFi.Report uses cookies and similar technologies for the following purposes:

2.1 Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the website to function properly. They enable basic features like:

  • Maintaining security during your browsing session
  • Enabling core functionality of the analysis tool
  • Loading website resources efficiently

You cannot opt-out of essential cookies as they are required for the website to work.

2.2 Analytics Cookies (Google Analytics)

We use Google Analytics (GA4) to understand how visitors interact with our website. These cookies help us:

  • Count visitors and understand which pages are most popular
  • See how visitors navigate through the site
  • Identify technical issues and improve performance
  • Understand which content is most useful

Important: Google Analytics collects aggregated, anonymized data. We do not use it to identify individual users or track you across other websites.

2.3 Advertising Cookies (Google AdSense)

We use Google AdSense to display advertisements on our website. Google AdSense may set cookies to:

  • Show relevant advertisements based on your browsing activity
  • Measure ad performance and engagement
  • Prevent the same ad from appearing too frequently
  • Report on ad campaign effectiveness

Note: You can control personalized advertising through Google Ad Settings.

4. Third-Party Cookies

WiFi.Report uses the following third-party services that set their own cookies:

4.1 Google Analytics

Google Analytics is a web analytics service provided by Google LLC. It uses cookies to track and report website traffic. The information collected is aggregated and anonymized. Google's use of this data is governed by their privacy policy: Google Privacy Policy

4.2 Google AdSense

Google AdSense is an advertising service provided by Google LLC. It uses cookies to serve ads based on your browsing activity across websites. You can manage your ad preferences at Google Ad Settings and opt out of personalized advertising. Learn more: Google Advertising Policies

4.3 IP Geolocation Services

When you run a WiFi analysis, we may use third-party IP geolocation services (such as ipapi.co) to provide approximate location information based on your IP address. These services may set their own cookies. This data is processed locally in your browser and is not sent to our servers.

5. Local Storage and Other Technologies

In addition to cookies, WiFi.Report uses:

5.1 Local Storage

HTML5 local storage allows us to store data locally in your browser. We use this to:

  • Cache resources for faster loading
  • Store temporary analysis results
  • Remember your preferences

5.2 Session Storage

Session storage is similar to local storage but is cleared when you close your browser. We use this for temporary data during your browsing session.

6. How to Control Cookies

You have several options to control and manage cookies:

6.1 Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can:

  • View what cookies are stored and delete them individually
  • Block third-party cookies
  • Block all cookies (this may affect website functionality)
  • Delete all cookies when you close your browser

Here's how to manage cookies in popular browsers:

  • Chrome: Settings > Privacy and security > Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Settings > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Cookies and website data
  • Edge: Settings > Cookies and site permissions > Cookies and site data

6.2 Google Ad Settings

You can control how Google uses cookies for advertising:

  • Visit Google Ad Settings to manage personalized ads
  • Opt out of personalized advertising while still seeing ads
  • Control which advertisers can show you ads

6.3 Do Not Track

Some browsers have a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature that sends a signal to websites requesting not to be tracked. While Google Analytics and AdSense may still function, you can use browser extensions and settings to further limit tracking.

7. Cookie Consent for Different Regions

7.1 European Union (GDPR)

If you're in the EU, we comply with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the ePrivacy Directive. We:

  • Obtain your consent before setting non-essential cookies
  • Provide clear information about cookie usage
  • Allow you to withdraw consent at any time
  • Respect your privacy rights

7.2 California (CCPA)

For California residents, we comply with the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA). While cookies are not typically considered "personal information" under CCPA, we provide transparency about their use.

7.3 Other Regions

We follow best practices for cookie usage regardless of your location and comply with applicable local laws.

8. What We DON'T Track

It's important to understand what WiFi.Report does NOT do:

  • Your Network Data: All WiFi analysis is performed 100% locally in your browser. Your network information, IP addresses, speed test results, and security analysis never leave your device or get sent to our servers.
  • Personal Identification: We don't collect names, email addresses, phone numbers, or other personal identifiers (unless you voluntarily contact us).
  • Cross-Site Tracking: We don't track your browsing activity across other websites.
  • Behavioral Profiling: We don't build detailed user profiles or sell your data to third parties.
  • Social Media Tracking: We don't embed social media tracking pixels.
  • Keystroke or Mouse Tracking: We don't record your keystrokes or mouse movements.
  • Selling Data: We never sell, rent, or share your personal information with third parties for marketing purposes.

Privacy First: WiFi.Report was built with privacy as a core principle. The cookies we use are for basic functionality, understanding aggregate site usage, and serving ads - not for invasive tracking.

9. How Long Do Cookies Last?

The duration of cookies varies based on their purpose:

  • Session Cookies: Deleted when you close your browser
  • Google Analytics: Up to 2 years
  • Google AdSense: Varies by cookie type, typically up to 2 years
  • Local Storage: Persistent until manually cleared

You can delete all cookies at any time through your browser settings.

10. Updates to Cookie Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in:

  • Cookie technologies we use
  • Legal requirements
  • Our business practices

We will notify you of significant changes by updating the "Last Updated" date at the top of this policy.

11. Impact of Blocking Cookies

While you can block or delete cookies, this may affect your experience on WiFi.Report:

  • The website will still function - core WiFi analysis works without cookies
  • Analysis results may not be saved between sessions
  • We won't be able to understand which features are most useful
  • You may see less relevant advertisements

Good News: The core WiFi analysis functionality works entirely in your browser and doesn't require any cookies to function.

12. Children's Privacy

WiFi.Report does not knowingly set cookies on devices used by children under 13. If you believe a child has used our website, you can delete cookies through browser settings.
