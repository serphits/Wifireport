Cookie Policy

Last updated: December 22, 2025

Quick Summary: WiFi.Report uses minimal cookies, primarily to remember your cookie consent choice and improve your experience. We don't use cookies for tracking or advertising purposes.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, smartphone, or tablet) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners.

Cookies can be "persistent" (remaining on your device for a set period) or "session" (deleted when you close your browser).

2. How We Use Cookies

WiFi.Report uses cookies and similar technologies for the following purposes:

2.1 Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the website to function properly. They enable basic features like:

  • Remembering your cookie consent preferences
  • Maintaining security during your browsing session
  • Enabling core functionality of the analysis tool

You cannot opt-out of essential cookies as they are required for the website to work.

2.2 Functionality Cookies

These cookies allow the website to remember choices you make and provide enhanced features:

  • Saving your analysis results temporarily for easy access
  • Remembering your preferences for future visits
  • Optimizing performance by caching resources

2.3 Analytics Cookies

We may use analytics cookies to understand how visitors interact with our website. These help us:

  • Count visitors and understand which pages are most popular
  • See how visitors move around the site
  • Identify and fix technical issues
  • Improve the overall user experience

Note: If we use analytics, we use privacy-focused solutions that don't track individual users.

4. Third-Party Cookies

WiFi.Report may use limited third-party services that may set their own cookies:

4.3 IP Geolocation Services

When you run a WiFi analysis, we may use third-party IP geolocation services (such as ipapi.co) to provide approximate location information. These services may set their own cookies. Please refer to their privacy policies for more information.

4.4 Content Delivery Networks (CDN)

We may use CDNs to deliver website resources faster. These services may set cookies for performance and security purposes.

5. Local Storage and Other Technologies

In addition to cookies, WiFi.Report uses:

5.1 Local Storage

HTML5 local storage allows us to store data locally in your browser. We use this to:

  • Cache resources for faster loading
  • Store temporary analysis results
  • Remember your preferences

5.2 Session Storage

Session storage is similar to local storage but is cleared when you close your browser. We use this for temporary data during your browsing session.

6. How to Control Cookies

You have several options to control and manage cookies:

6.1 Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can:

  • View what cookies are stored and delete them individually
  • Block third-party cookies
  • Block all cookies (this may affect website functionality)
  • Delete all cookies when you close your browser

Here's how to manage cookies in popular browsers:

  • Chrome: Settings > Privacy and security > Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Settings > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Cookies and website data
  • Edge: Settings > Cookies and site permissions > Cookies and site data

6.2 Our Cookie Banner

When you first visit WiFi.Report, you'll see a cookie banner that allows you to:

  • Accept all cookies
  • Decline non-essential cookies
  • Learn more about our cookie practices

Your choice will be remembered for future visits.

6.3 Do Not Track

WiFi.Report respects the "Do Not Track" (DNT) browser setting. If you enable DNT, we will respect your choice and limit data collection where possible.

7. Cookie Consent for Different Regions

7.1 European Union (GDPR)

If you're in the EU, we comply with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the ePrivacy Directive. We:

  • Obtain your consent before setting non-essential cookies
  • Provide clear information about cookie usage
  • Allow you to withdraw consent at any time
  • Respect your privacy rights

7.2 California (CCPA)

For California residents, we comply with the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA). While cookies are not typically considered "personal information" under CCPA, we provide transparency about their use.

7.3 Other Regions

We follow best practices for cookie usage regardless of your location and comply with applicable local laws.

8. Why We Don't Use Certain Cookies

WiFi.Report intentionally avoids certain types of cookies:

  • Advertising Cookies: We don't use cookies for targeted advertising
  • Social Media Cookies: We don't embed social media tracking pixels
  • Cross-Site Tracking: We don't participate in cross-site tracking networks
  • Behavioral Profiling: We don't build user profiles for marketing purposes

9. How Long Do Cookies Last?

The duration of cookies varies based on their purpose:

  • Session Cookies: Deleted when you close your browser
  • Short-term Cookies: Last 24 hours to 30 days
  • Long-term Cookies: Last up to 1 year (primarily consent cookies)

You can delete all cookies at any time through your browser settings.

10. Updates to Cookie Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in:

  • Cookie technologies we use
  • Legal requirements
  • Our business practices

We will notify you of significant changes by updating the "Last Updated" date at the top of this policy.

11. Impact of Blocking Cookies

While you can block or delete cookies, this may affect your experience on WiFi.Report:

  • You may see the cookie banner on every visit
  • Analysis results may not be saved between sessions
  • Some features may not work as expected
  • The website may load more slowly

Essential cookies cannot be blocked without breaking core functionality.

12. Children's Privacy

WiFi.Report does not knowingly set cookies on devices used by children under 13. If you believe a child has used our website, you can delete cookies through browser settings.

13. Contact Us About Cookies

If you have questions about our use of cookies, please contact us at:

  • Email: privacy@wifi.report
  • Website: https://wifi.report

Your Control: At WiFi.Report, we believe you should have full control over your data and privacy. That's why we use minimal cookies, provide clear choices, and respect your preferences. You can always delete cookies or opt-out of non-essential ones without losing access to our core service.
